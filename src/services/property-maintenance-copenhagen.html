Clearer control over what’s happening in your units

Instead of coordinating multiple small tasks, following up with tenants, and trying to piece together what was actually done, you get a single point of contact and a clear outcome for each visit.

How Northstar works

  • You send a request or a list of issues
  • We coordinate access directly with tenants if needed
  • The work gets done in one or more visits
  • You receive a simple summary with photos

What you get after each visit

After each visit, you receive a short update that makes it easy to track what changed without chasing the details yourself.

  • Reference to the specific apartment, unit, or common area
  • Tasks completed during the visit
  • Materials or parts replaced, if relevant
  • Photos where relevant
  • Clear note on follow-up or escalation

Example visit update

Unit: Apt 3B
Status: Ready
Tasks completed: Tightened kitchen handle, replaced light bulb, checked lock hardware
Materials used: 1x bulb
Follow-up: None

This is typically useful for

  • Property managers and landlords handling recurring maintenance across units
  • Furnished portfolios and small rental buildings with grouped visits
  • Rental operators with multiple units and ongoing coordination
  • Guest-facing spaces when the work is operational, not renovation-led

Pricing orientation

Most jobs are quoted from photos, access notes, and scope. The scope is usually clarified first, then the most practical visit structure is proposed.

  • Single-unit visits are quoted based on scope and access.
  • Grouped visits are usually more efficient when multiple units are handled together.
  • Recurring setups are priced around the actual workload, not an abstract fixed fee.

What this is (and what it isn’t)

This is not a full facility management service or an emergency line. It’s structured, practical support for ongoing maintenance and small issues.

  • No 24/7 emergency service
  • No licensed electrical or plumbing work
  • No large renovations or construction

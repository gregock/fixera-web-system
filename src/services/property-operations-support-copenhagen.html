How it works in practice

In property operations, small issues are spread across units, common areas, and shifting access windows. This support model consolidates recurring execution so coordination and follow-up become simpler.

  • Multiple small issues distributed across several apartments.
  • Time spent coordinating between different specialized vendors.
  • Recurring tasks during inspections and move-in/move-out handovers.
  • Lack of a stable local contact for daily practical execution.
  • Need for clear status updates after each round.

The goal is simple: close more tasks per round, maintain a clear overview, and keep operations predictable across your portfolio.

Execution & Delivery

What you get after each visit

After each visit, you receive a concise, structured update so you know exactly what was completed and what requires follow-up.

  • Reference to the specific apartment or unit
  • Tasks completed during the visit
  • Materials or parts replaced (if relevant)
  • Photos where relevant
  • Clear note on follow-up or escalation

This allows you to track status across multiple units without having to chase updates or coordinate multiple vendors.

Operational details

Most operators want a few practical points clarified before support starts.

  • How is access handled across units? Access can be handled via physical keys, lockboxes, KeyNest, or temporary smart lock workflows, including common Copenhagen systems like Ruko and Yale.
  • How are vacant units and tenant visits handled? Vacant units can be handled in access windows, and when tenant access is required, communication is kept practical and professional.
  • How are materials and replacements handled? Basic consumables and common spare parts can be handled during the visit, with sourcing of specific parts as needed and documentation of usage in the update.
  • What preventative checks are included? Routine checks can include early leak detection, verification of critical installations, and confirming that key inventory and assets are functional.
  • What does the post-visit update contain? Each round concludes with a structured update including photos, tasks completed, follow-up notes, and escalation when conditions fall outside the scope.

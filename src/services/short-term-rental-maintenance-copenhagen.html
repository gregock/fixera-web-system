How this works in practice

Turnovers run under checkout/check-in pressure, usually in the 11:00-15:00 window. Issues are often discovered after cleaning when there is limited time to react and the operator is not on site.

  • Checkout is done, check-in is coming, and the window is short.
  • The cleaner reports a stay-critical issue during turnover.
  • There is limited time to access the apartment and fix what is actually feasible.
  • Work is scheduled around real availability, not a guaranteed timing promise.
  • Small furnished portfolios need practical support without extra coordination.

The goal is to reduce unresolved issues before arrival while keeping expectations realistic around timing.

What Fixera handles

  • Stay-critical readiness checks between bookings, focused on issues that can impact guest experience at check-in.
  • Post-checkout issue review for what should be addressed before the next stay when timing allows, not generic handyman backlog items.
  • Small repairs and maintenance related to core functionality and comfort, including worn handles, hooks, catches and similar small parts that affect guest use.
  • Installation services and re-installations that restore basic in-unit usability after damage, replacement needs or guest turnover.
  • Visual-standard and handover-critical resets (curtain rails, lamps, shelves and similar), including minor end-of-tenancy fixes.
  • Comfort-focused furniture adjustments, tightening and minor replacements in furnished units where immediate guest use is affected.
  • Access and entry-device troubleshooting for smoother arrivals, including smart lock routines where relevant.
  • Practical stay-readiness coordination across one unit or a manageable group of apartments, based on real access windows and feasible scope.

Why this works well for operators

  • One visit to close multiple small issues between stays.
  • Clear updates without needing to be on site.
  • Less coordination across cleaning, maintenance and access.

Pricing orientation

Most jobs are quoted from photos, access notes and scope. As a general guide, single-unit reset visits usually start from 600–900 DKK, while grouped visits or broader between-stay task lists are priced based on time, access and how many issues can be closed in one round.

  • Single-unit reset visits for one furnished apartment.
  • Grouped visits for a small manageable set of units.
  • Clear confirmation of what fits in one visit and what needs follow-up.

What this service is, and is not

  • This is stay-critical support for furnished apartments between bookings.
  • This covers practical fixes that affect check-in, guest comfort, or apartment readiness.
  • This is not full renovation or large-scale project work.
  • This is not licensed electrical or plumbing installation.
  • For mixed portfolios or broader operations, route through property operations support.

What you get after each visit

After each visit, you receive a clear post-visit update so operators can see what was completed, what still needs follow-up, and what should be scheduled next.

  • Clear post-visit status summary for the unit or visit batch.
  • Completed work listed clearly for each addressed issue.
  • Follow-up items called out, including parts or additional work if needed.
  • Notes on access limits, material constraints, or site conditions when relevant.
  • Recommended next step based on current scope and access.
  • Updates grouped by apartment when multiple units are handled.

Example visit update

Unit: Apt 4C
Status: Ready
Completed: Tightened curtain rail, replaced bulb, checked lock hardware
Follow-up: None
Notes: Access via key box, no extra materials needed

Simple process

  1. A cleaner or operator flags a stay-critical issue that can affect check-in or guest comfort.
  2. You send photos, access details, and timing context for the next check-in window.
  3. Fixera evaluates what is realistically feasible in scope and schedules the visit around that context.
  4. After the visit, you receive a clear update on what was completed and what, if anything, needs follow-up.
